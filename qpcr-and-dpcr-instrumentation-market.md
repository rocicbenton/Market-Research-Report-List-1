<p><p>Quantitative Polymerase Chain Reaction (qPCR) and Digital Polymerase Chain Reaction (dPCR) are advanced molecular biology techniques used for the amplification and quantification of DNA. qPCR allows for the simultaneous quantification of DNA during the amplification process, providing real-time data on nucleic acid concentrations. In contrast, dPCR offers precise quantitative measurements by partitioning samples into thousands of individual reactions, improving sensitivity and accuracy.</p><p>The qPCR and dPCR instrumentation market is experiencing significant growth driven by factors such as increasing demand for genetic testing, advancements in genomic research, and the rise of personalized medicine.
