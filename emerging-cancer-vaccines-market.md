<p><p>The emerging cancer vaccines market is primarily categorized into non-genetic and genetic vaccines. Non-genetic vaccines include traditional approaches like protein-based or peptide vaccines, which stimulate the immune response using tumor antigens. Genetic vaccines, on the other hand, involve the introduction of genetic material encoding tumor antigens, enabling the body to produce these antigens and elicit a stronger immune response. Both types aim to enhance the body's ability to identify and attack cancer cells, playing a crucial role in cancer immunotherapy advancements.</p></p>
